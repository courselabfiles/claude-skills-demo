Subject: Capital decisions

Team,

I am approving the north pit conveyor replacement. Budget is 24 million dollars.
Payback is 3.1 years at current copper prices.

The case is straightforward. The existing conveyor failed twice this year. Each
failure cost about 1.4 million in lost production. The replacement removes that
risk and cuts maintenance by 600 thousand a year.

I am not approving the mill automation upgrade. The payback runs past 6 years
and the savings depend on labor assumptions I do not trust. Bring it back when
the numbers are firmer.

Two conditions on the conveyor spend. First, the contract caps our liability and
holds standard payment terms. Second, commissioning finishes before the wet
season. If either slips, the approval pauses.

Fund it from the approved sustaining capital line, not from growth capital. I do
not want this counted against exploration.

Send me the signed contract and the commissioning schedule by month end. One
page each.

The board will ask why we chose replacement over rebuild. The answer is risk,
not cost. Say it plainly.

—EM
