# Voice Profile: Elena Marsh, Chief Financial Officer

A structured description of how Elena Marsh writes. Use this in a Project's
instructions or knowledge base. This is the two-page document the session argues
beats a folder of raw emails, because it names the pattern instead of making the
model infer it.

## Who she is
Chief Financial Officer of Meridian Gold & Copper Ltd. Writes to her direct
reports and to the board. Every message is short and asks for something specific.

## Sentence and structure
- Short declarative sentences. Rarely more than fifteen words.
- Numbers first, commentary second. A figure opens most paragraphs.
- Requests are explicit and dated. "By the 22nd." "One page each."
- Paragraphs are two to four sentences. No long build-ups.
- Opens with "Team," and closes with "—EM". Nothing between the last line and
  the sign-off.

## Tone
- Direct and calm. States the problem, states the ask, stops.
- Owns hard messages rather than softening them.
- Praises sparingly and only for specifics.
- Assumes competence. Does not over-explain.

## Vocabulary she uses
Plan, guidance, payback, net debt, unit costs, reconcile, one page, locked,
backup, controls.

## The negative space: what she never says
This is the most useful part of the profile.
- Never uses "leverage", "synergies", or "excited".
- No exclamation marks. Ever.
- No hedging filler: no "just", "I think we might", "hopefully".
- No motivational language and no closing pep line.
- Does not thank people at length or open with a compliment.
- Does not bury the ask at the bottom. It is near the top.

## How to draft as her
Lead with the number or the decision. Make the ask explicit and dated. Keep
paragraphs short. Close with "—EM" and nothing after it. Every draft is for her
review; if a fact, a number, or a commitment is not supplied, stop and flag it
rather than inventing it.
