# Meridian Gold & Copper Ltd Reports Second Quarter Results

[CITY], [DATE]. Meridian Gold & Copper Ltd today reported its operating and
financial results for the second quarter.

Gold production for the quarter was 121,400 ounces of gold, in line with the
company's plan for the period. Copper production reached 9,800 tonnes of copper,
ahead of the internal target. The all-in sustaining cost for gold was $13,420
per ounce, which the company held within its full-year guidance range.

Quarterly revenue was $486.2 million, supported by firm realized prices for both
metals. Cash generated from operations funded continued debt reduction, and the
company ended the quarter with a stronger balance sheet.

"The second quarter delivered steady output across both metals," said the Chief
Financial Officer. "We believe the cost discipline shown this quarter positions
the business well for the second half."

Safety performance remained a priority. The operation recorded 2 lost-time
injuries during the quarter, with a total recordable injury frequency rate of
0.38 per million hours worked. The company continues to target zero harm across
all sites.

Mill throughput at the north pit was affected by a short maintenance outage in
the quarter, while higher grades at the south pit offset most of the impact. The
company completed approximately 90 percent of its planned sustaining capital
program for the period, including progress on the north pit conveyor
replacement.

Looking ahead, Meridian expects production in the second half to be weighted
toward the fourth quarter, consistent with the mine plan. The company reaffirmed
its full-year guidance for gold and copper production, unit costs, and capital
expenditure.
